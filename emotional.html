Emotional Temperature Map Characteristics:-

    1. Dynamic: Emotions can shift and change over time

    2. Context-dependent: Emotions can be influenced by situation and context

    3. Individualized: Emotional experiences and temperature ranges may vary across individuals

    4. Multidimensional: Emotions can have multiple components (e.g., cognitive, physiological, behavioral)

    Using the Emotional Temperature Map:-

    1. Self-reflection: Identify and understand your emotional states.

    2. Emotional labeling: Label and acknowledge emotions.

    3. Emotional regulation: Manage and modulate emotions.

    4. Communication: Effectively express and understand emotions in relationships.

    Emotional Temperature Map Categories:-

    1. Positive Emotions: Warm Zone (50-69°C) - Happiness, Joy, Contentment.

    2. Negative Emotions: Hot Zone (80-89°C) and Cold Zone (-10°C to -19°C) - Anger, Fear, Sadness.

    3. Neutral Emotions: Neutral Zone (20-39°C) - Balance, Indifference, Stability.

    Emotional Temperature Map and Well-being.

    1. Emotional Balance: Achieving balance and stability in emotional states.

    2. Emotional Resilience: Developing coping skills and resilience.

    3. Emotional Intelligence: Understanding and managing emotions effectively.
